The Crash of 1983

The industry almost ended in 1983 due to a market flooded with low-quality games like E.T.

The 1980s Revolution

The 1980s was the decade that defined modern gaming. Following the video game crash of 1983, Nintendo released the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), which revitalized the home console market with hits like Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda.

This era introduced 8-bit graphics, scrolling backgrounds, and complex musical scores. It also saw the birth of the "Console Wars" as Sega challenged Nintendo with the Master System and later the 16-bit Genesis at the very end of the decade.

Portable gaming also took a massive leap forward in 1989 with the release of the Nintendo Game Boy, proving that players wanted to take their adventures on the go.
